Pancakes taste better in the snow

by Joseph Wilcken

Every Saturday during the Spring, Summer, and Fall months, the city council of Preston hosts a pancake breakfast for the public in the city park pavilion. With the early snowfall this year, many individuals thought that the city would cancel the pancake breakfast because of the cold weather. Perhaps no one told the city council, because they were outside just as they planned with their extension cords, electric griddles, and plenty of pancake mix.

The local turnout was small at 9:00am, however that soon changed as one of the local hardware store owners contributed some outdoor heaters to the cause. With the added warmth the crowd continued to grow. Ultimately, the city council reported that the attendance was only 83% of their average attendance rate. One local man when asked said he was grateful that this tradition wasn’t cut short because of the weather. Said he, “My kids love this pancake breakfast and were very upset when I told them I didn’t think it would happen today. I think this was a huge success. The city should consider making pancakes year-round.”
