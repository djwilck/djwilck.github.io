Visual Hierarchy

Brilliant Earth

brilliantearth.com

As a marketing website, Brilliant Earth is very good at using visual hierarchy to draw in the consumers and direct their attention. When I searched the internet for engagement rings and clicked on their website the very first thing I saw was a large beautiful diamond on a finger, followed immediately by the button right below it that said "Shop Rings". My first two thoughts from the site were naturally, there are the rings, and this is how I can buy them. I then noticed the Title which confirmed that the gem was a diamond and not another jewel, and then the last thing my eyes were drawn to was the description. The position and size of the ring in the image made sure it was the first thing noticed on the website. The next largest thing in the image was the button. By placing it right below the jewel, it was likely to be the second or third thing noticed.

Fitt's Law

eBay

ebay.com

The most common places for buttons to be placed on a phone is in the lower half of the screen. As eBay is currently advertising their fall seasonal products, they have placed an array of decent-sized buttons right on their main page at thumb height. This is in accordance with Fitt's law, because they have made their buttons close enough the thumb of the user and made them large enough that they are easy to click without being obnoxious. They have made it much easier for the user to click on those advertised products than to scroll through the categories at the top of the screen. Therefore, they have increased the chance of a casual browser to view their featured products.

Alignment

Forbes

forbes.com

As a well-known professional business, it is no surprise that Forbes has a very clean website design. The content is horizontally aligned throughout the website. You can see clearly that the left edge is clear with the images, headings, and number of readers. Even the arrows of increase and decrease line up clearly and the accompanying headlines are aligned. It clearly defines what content is related and separates what is unrelated.
